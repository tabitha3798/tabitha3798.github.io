Q-matrix Study

Cognitive Diagnostic Assessment & Q-matrix

Unlike classical tests, CDA is designed for analyzing examtakers' abilities in diverse demensions, so they can realize their strengh and weakness from the results. First, test developers decide attributes that they want to examine, and develop items that can reveal those attributes.

Q-matrix is an incidence matrix that shows the relationship between items and attributes.

What if the Q-matrix is wrong?

True Q-matrix is essential for exact estimation. If the Q-matrix is wrong, whole estimation process can be affected, i.e., item parameters and attribute patterns can be estimated wrong.

My research is about how to get true Q-matrix or validate given Q-matrix.

Related studies (Click the project title to visit github page!)

Bayesian Estimation for Q-matrix of CDM

Currently, several bayesian approaches for estimating Q-matrix of cognitive diagnostic models existed, but there are not enough research that consider proposed Q-matrix from experts or include hirarchy information. I want to design a bayesian model that can reflect educational expert's knowledge more.

Q-matrix Validation Method & AVERSS

From the idea of the non-parametric Q-matrix refinement method using RSS, this study suggested a validation method that judge whether the Q-matrix given by educational experts is true or not. The average of RSS, AVERSS, was used for validation index.

Review of Delta Method

The sequential delta method is the most common used Q-matrix method, and enlarged to various versions, even though it still contains many weaknesses. I analyzed theoretical problems of this method, and conducted simulation study to check it's accuracy with my colleague Raeyoung Kim.
