Q-matrix Study

Cognitive Diagnostic Assessment & Q-matrix

Unlike classical tests, CDA is designed for analyzing examtakers' abilities in diverse demensions, so they can realize their strengh and weakness from the results. So, the objective of CDA is not only to examine student's level, but also to help students for next study. Normally, in the process of the development of CDA, test developers decide attributes that they want to examine, and develop items that can reveal those attributes.

Q-matrix is an incidence matrix that shows the relationship between items and attributes.

What if the Q-matrix is Wrong?

True Q-matrix is essential for exact estimation. If the Q-matrix is wrong, whole estimation process can be affected, i.e., item parameters and attribute patterns can be estimated wrong. My research is about how to get true Q-matrix or validate given Q-matrix.

Experts' Knowledge vs Data

If there is no validation process for the original Q-matrix suggested by humen, Q-matrix can be surgective, and it is hard to claim the validity of Q-matrix. On the other hand, if Q-matrix is determined by only data, meanings of attributes can differ by the original meanings, and interpretation can be a problem. The final goal of my research is developing a validation process reflecting experts' knowledge sufficiently that can be useful for real educational situation.

Bayesian Estimation for Q-matrix of CDM

Currently, several bayesian approaches (Chung(2014), Culpepper(2015), Chen(2018)) for estimating Q-matrix of cognitive diagnostic models existed, but there are not enough research that consider proposed Q-matrix from experts or include hirarchy information.
I want to design a bayesian model that can reflect real educational situation more. In learning situations, each skills are usually inextricably linked, and there exists a hierarchy structure between attributes. It is already known that with the hierarchy information, the estimation process can be more accurately and efficiently.

Q-matrix Validation Method & AVERSS

Usually, confirmatory studies about Q-matrix are consist of refinement processes. However, those processes are not appropriate for many situations, becuase the results of refinement are often not matched to meanings of attributes, which can cause interpreation problems.
So, I wanted to build a validation method with pass-fail format. From the idea of the non-parametric Q-matrix refinement method using RSS, this study suggested a validation method that judge whether the suggested Q-matrix by educational experts is true or not. The average of RSS, AVERSS, was used for validation index.

Review of Delta Method

The sequential delta method, which was suggested by de la torre(2008), is the most commonly used confirmatory Q-matrix method, and enlarged to various versions. However, I noticed several weaknesses of it when I adapted this method into the TIMSS(2003) study.
To improve, I analyzed theoretical problems of this method, and conducted simulation study to check it's accuracy under DINA and DINO models with my colleague Raeyoung Kim.
